Known Issues

  • There are some areas near the time-boundary that should clearly be included within the time-boundary
  • I am in the middle of the Atlantic Ocean on top of "Ace Lock Service Inc"
  • I am near a large body of water or am in a relatively isolated area, and the shape is completely wonky

Is your issue not included in this list? Contact us.

Explanations



There are points in this image that seem like they should be included in the specified time-boundary, particularly, the highway in the top, near Hazel Dell South.

The shape is calculated using 20 points distributed radially the center point. Because no point sits on that road but rather near the road, the furthest point you can get to on that road is not included in the shape calculation. Consequently, the resulting shape is just an estimate, and this issue should be taken into account when using the distance mapper.



This bizarre result seems to happen when small areas surrounded by water are used in the address. An example is Oahu, Hawaii. This is a known issue and we are working to address it.



This crazy shape business seems to occur when the center point is a relatively isolated area. For instance, there are very few roads one can take out of Anchorage, AK, for 3 hours. Very few of the points used in the calculation were actually accessible by driving, so only a few of them could be used to draw the shape, resulting in a bizarre, unhelpful time-boundary. This is a known issue and we are working to address it.
